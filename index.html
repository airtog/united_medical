Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

We're happy to help with anything — but here are answers to the questions we hear most often.

What is the I-693 form and why do I need it?

Form I-693 is the official Report of Medical Examination and Vaccination Record required by USCIS. It must be completed by a designated Civil Surgeon and submitted as part of your green card (adjustment of status) application. Without it, your application cannot be processed.

How long does the exam take?

The in-office exam typically takes about 30 minutes. You'll receive your sealed I-693 form once all lab results are returned, which is usually within 5–7 business days.

Why is your exam $599 when other places charge less?

Most practices that charge less do not include the required bloodwork and lab tests. Patients end up being sent to Quest Diagnostics or similar labs where those tests alone can cost over $1,000. Our $599 includes everything — exam, labs, blood tests, and sealed I-693 form — all done in one visit under one roof. No surprises.

Do I need to fast before my appointment?

No fasting is required for the I-693 exam. You can eat and drink normally before your visit.

What vaccinations are required?

USCIS requires certain vaccinations including MMR (measles, mumps, rubella), Tdap (tetanus, diphtheria, pertussis), varicella, influenza (seasonal), COVID-19, and others depending on your age. We'll review your records and let you know exactly what you need.

Do you accept insurance?

The I-693 exam is a flat-fee service at $599. While most insurance plans do not cover immigration medical exams, we recommend checking with your provider. We accept cash, credit cards, and all major payment methods.

How long is the I-693 form valid?

The I-693 form is valid for 2 years from the date the civil surgeon signs it. We recommend completing your exam as close to your USCIS filing date as possible to maximize validity.

Can I bring a family member or translator?

Absolutely. You're welcome to bring someone for support. Our staff also speaks Spanish to help you feel comfortable throughout your visit.

Are chest X-rays included?

Chest X-rays are not performed in our office, but if one is required for your exam, we'll refer you to a nearby partner facility where you can get a quick appointment.
